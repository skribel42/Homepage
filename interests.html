Offensive Security

This is where I want to go. I've already performed small-scale penetration tests and loved every second of it. The challenge, the methodology, the satisfaction of finding something before a real attacker does — it's addictive.

I stay on top of the latest CVEs and vulnerabilities because understanding what's out there is half the battle. Working toward OSCP with the full support of my employer, which makes the grind a lot more rewarding.

Penetration Testing Red Teaming TryHackMe OSCP (in progress) CVE Tracking CTFs

The MSP Life

Working at an MSP means no two days look the same. One day you're servicing systems for a dental office — panoramic X-ray machines and all — and the next you're supporting infrastructure for government contractors involved in pyrotechnics. You learn to adapt fast.

I genuinely love my job and the variety it brings. Getting to build an SIEM solution from the ground up is the kind of project that doesn't come along often, and I'm here for every bit of it. Patch management, endpoint security, Microsoft 365, RMM — I've touched all of it.

SIEM Patch Management Endpoint Security Microsoft 365 RMM

Homelabbing

My homelab is where I break things on purpose. Running a 3-node mini PC cluster with virtualization, networking, DNS, and high availability — all to learn hands-on what documentation can't teach.

Virtualization Linux Networking Self-hosting
